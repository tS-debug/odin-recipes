Bibimbap

Description

Korean bibimbap, along with kimchi, takes its place among the favored foods in Korean cuisine. Meaning mixed rice, bibimbap is a popular meal consisting of white rice topped with vegetables, beef, a whole egg, and gochujang (red chili pepper paste). You can substitute with soy sauce or Sriracha (rooster sauce) for those who cannot handle the spiciness, like our children.

Ingredients

  • Cucumber
  • Gochujang
  • Soy Sauce
  • Olive Oil
  • Garlic cloves
  • Eggs
  • Beef top round steak
  • Cooked white rice
  • Sesame oil

Steps

  1. Stir the cucumber pieces and gochujang paste in a bowl and set aside.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet and cook the veggies.
  3. Brown the beef in a skillet over medium heat at about 5 minutes per side.
  4. Heat the remaining olive oil and fry the eggs in it.
  5. Divide cooked rice into serving bowls appropriately and put a little bit of everything including beef, veggies, eggs, and cucumber mixture.
